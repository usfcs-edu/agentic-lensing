Prof. Xiaosheng Huang (USF, also LBNL Physics Division affiliate) leads a research program that uses machine learning to discover strong gravitational lenses in wide-area imaging surveys, then characterizes them with HST imaging, Keck NIRES NIR spectroscopy, VLT/MUSE IFU spectroscopy, and DESI spectroscopy.

**Why:** Strong lenses enable H₀ measurement via time-delay cosmography, dark-matter substructure detection (CDM tests at low halo mass), and high-redshift galaxy magnification. The bottleneck is candidate discovery and visual-inspection grading — exactly where ML and (now) agentic AI can scale the program.

**How to apply:** When discussing the program, organize around its five discovery modalities (ResNet imaging cutouts; ResNet+EfficientNet+meta-learner ensemble; difference-imaging for transients; autocorrelation for lensed quasars; pairwise spectroscopic search for "dimple lenses") and three pillars (Discover → Characterize → Cosmologize). The umbrella program is the **DESI Strong Lens Foundry** with papers I (HST imaging + GIGA-Lens modeling), II (DESI spectroscopy), III (Keck NIRES), IV (VLT/MUSE). Cumulative candidate count is ~3,500-5,500 across DR9/DR10.

Key collaborators: Christopher Storfer, Jose Carlos Inchausti, William Sheu, Yuan-Ming Hsu, Andi Gu, Aleksandar Cikota, C. Dawes, Ethan Silver, Emerald Lis (Lin), Shirihan Agarwal, Stephen Bailey (LBNL), David Schlegel (LBNL).
