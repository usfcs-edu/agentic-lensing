WS3 of the build-out (2026-06-05/07) DONE. Local model-size scaling ladder on 2×L4 to test "scale is the lever" for galaxy redshift good-z. Design hardened via a Workflow methodology review (4 verifiers): dropped the data axis (<1 OOM, low power + submanifest→val leakage risk), added SEED REPLICATION at endpoints for noise bars, pre-specified criteria (good_z slope ≥ +0.04/decade ∧ R²>0.5 ∧ > seed-noise), documented the fixed-8k DOWNWARD bias + the ignition-threshold caveat.

Harness (additive, default-preserving): train_transformer persists `model_config` (d_model/layers/heads/max_seq_len) in every ckpt; eval tools (eval_per_class/probe_six_class/measure_equivariance) read it w/ 768/6/6/12 fallback (old ckpts byte-identical). New tools: `make_submanifest.py` (proportional stratified, seeded), `scaling_ladder.py` (parse eval output, compute_params via real construction, log-fit slope/R², plot), `run_scaling_ladder.sh` (driver, skip-if-done). All build+verified.

**RESULT (6 points, d∈{256,384,512,768}@seed42 + d∈{256,768}@seed123, full mix, 8k, eff-batch 64, n=4096 eval):** model-axis good_z = 0.222/0.221/0.232/0.215 across 13.1M→103.7M params → **slope −0.004/decade (R²=0.04): FLAT**, galaxy ceiling ~22%, galaxies 95-98% catastrophic at every size. Fails the +0.04 bar by 10×. **Seed-noise bars are the key finding:** d=768 seeds agree to 0.2pp (21.5 vs 21.3%); d=256 swings **12pp** (22.2 vs 10.2%; the s123 d=256 barely ignited — MWS 57% vs 9% catastrophic). So **scale buys training STABILITY/reliable ignition, not a higher galaxy good-z ceiling.** 8k points (~22%) sit below V1@15k (24.1%) → slope is a lower bound (8k undertrained).

**VERDICT: no resolvable local model-size benefit to galaxy good-z, but INCONCLUSIVE re NERSC** — the local ladder sits below the galaxy-ignition phase transition (cf. reference 8%→73.8% jump 219k→394k spectra). A flat LOCAL slope neither confirms nor refutes full-scale scaling; the precision verdict is genuinely deferred to the threshold-crossing NERSC runs. Net across Phases 15-18: every locally-reachable lever (architecture, equivariance, compression, model-size) is exhausted → the proposal's scale axis is the only untested lever, and it's untestable at 2×L4.
