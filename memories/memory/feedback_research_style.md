Greg wants research output that is (a) **thorough and deep**, (b) **concrete on the agentic-AI side** (named architectures, role decompositions, tool surfaces — not vague suggestions), and (c) **citation-verified** (URLs/claims grounded by WebFetch, not trusted from LLM output alone).

**Why:** He explicitly chose these on May 25 2026 when scoping the onboarding-report deliverable. He picked "Comprehensive (~30-50 pages)" for scope, "Concrete architectures" for proposal depth, and "Verify each via WebFetch" for citation handling. His agentic-systems expertise means survey-only writeups don't add value to him — concrete designs do.

**How to apply:** For any future research/writeup task on this project, default to: deep multi-section structure, named system proposals with diagrams/role decomposition/tool surface, and a verification pass against authoritative sources before citing. Avoid agent-confabulated 2025-2026 citations without a WebFetch grounding.
