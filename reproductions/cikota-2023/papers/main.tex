\begin{abstract}
We reproduce the GIGA-Lens \autocite{gu2022gigalens} lens model of the
Einstein cross DESI-253.2534+26.8843 from \citet{cikota2023einstein}.
The paper modeled an SDSS-$g$ image \emph{synthesized from a proprietary
VLT/MUSE data cube} ($\approx0.6\arcsec$ seeing); we omit the
spectroscopy entirely and instead refit the identical 31-parameter
mass-plus-light model (two singular-isothermal-ellipsoid lenses
$+$ external shear $+$ three S\'ersic components) on \emph{public} DESI
Legacy Surveys DR10 $g$-band imaging --- the same survey the system was
discovered in \autocite{huang2021discovery}. Our fit recovers the
four-image Einstein-cross geometry, a tangential critical ellipse
threading the four images, and a compact source inside the astroid
caustic, at a reduced $\chi^2 / \mathrm{px} = 0.90$. The headline
Einstein radius is $\thetaE = 2.10\arcsec\,(^{+0.04}_{-0.04})$ versus the
published $2.520\arcsec\,(^{+0.032}_{-0.031})$ ($-17\,\%$), with an
implied $\sigmaSIE = 347\,$km/s versus $379\pm2\,$km/s ($-8\,\%$) and a
total magnification $\mu_{\mathrm{tot}} = 7.0$ versus $10.47$. We show
quantitatively, via a PSF ablation, that the dominant cause of the
$\thetaE$ undershoot is \emph{seeing}: the public Legacy $g$ coadd PSF
has $\mathrm{FWHM}\approx1.35\arcsec$, more than twice the paper's
$0.6\arcsec$ MUSE-derived image. Refitting the same data with the
paper's $0.6\arcsec$ Gaussian PSF moves $\thetaE$ from $2.10\arcsec$ to
$2.28\arcsec$, closing roughly half the gap, with the residual gap
irreducible because the $1.35\arcsec$ seeing is baked into the public
pixels. The SIS$\to\sigma$ conversion itself is exact: feeding the paper's
$\thetaE = 2.520\arcsec$ and distances through our formula returns
$379.4\,$km/s, matching the paper's $379$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =============================================================================

\citet{cikota2023einstein} present the spectroscopic confirmation and
lens model of DESI-253.2534+26.8843, a strong-lensing system originally
flagged in the DESI Legacy Imaging Surveys
\autocite{huang2021discovery,dey2019legacy}. The system is a textbook
Einstein cross: a massive elliptical lens galaxy (L1) surrounded by four
blue images of a single background source arranged in a quad. The paper's
contributions are threefold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Spectroscopic confirmation} from a VLT/MUSE
      \autocite{bacon2010muse} integral-field data cube: lens redshift
      $z_{\mathrm{L1}} = 0.636$, source redshift $z_s = 2.597$ (a
      starburst galaxy), and a faint foreground galaxy (L2) at
      $z_{\mathrm{L2}} = 0.386$ projected near image~C.
\item \textbf{Lens modeling} with GIGA-Lens \autocite{gu2022gigalens}
      of a $g$-band image synthesized from the MUSE cube
      ($\mathrm{FWHM}\approx0.6\arcsec$). The mass model is L1 (SIE)
      $+$ L2 (SIE, treated as an effective subhalo) $+$ external shear;
      the light model is L1 (elliptical S\'ersic) $+$ L2 (spherical
      S\'ersic) $+$ source (elliptical S\'ersic): 31 free parameters in
      total.
\item \textbf{Headline measurements}: Einstein radius
      $\thetaE = 2.520\arcsec\,(^{+0.032}_{-0.031})$, corresponding to a
      velocity dispersion $\sigmaSIE = 379\pm2\,$km/s, with predicted
      per-image magnifications $(2.40, 3.23, 4.03, 0.81)$ for images
      $(A,B,C,D)$ summing to a total of $10.47$.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Scope of this reproduction.} We deliberately do \emph{not}
attempt the spectroscopy (stage 1): the VLT/MUSE cube is proprietary and
out of scope, so the redshifts are taken from the paper and used only as
context for the SIS$\to\sigma$ conversion. We \emph{do} reproduce the
lens-modeling stage (2), but on a harder footing than the paper: rather
than the $0.6\arcsec$ MUSE-derived $g$ image, we use the public DESI
Legacy DR10 $g$-band coadd, whose seeing ($\mathrm{FWHM}\approx1.35\arcsec$)
partially blends the four images. This makes the reproduction an honest
test of whether the GIGA-Lens model recovers the published geometry and
radius from \emph{independently obtained, lower-resolution, fully public}
data.

\section{Data}
% =============================================================================

We pull a $120\times120$ pixel ($0.262\arcsec$/px) $grz$ cutout centered
on (R.A., Dec.) $=(253.2534, +26.8843)$ from the public
\texttt{legacysurvey.org} cutout server at the \texttt{ls-dr10} layer,
together with the matching $g$-band coadd PSF
(\texttt{coadd-psf}). Script~01 (\texttt{01\_prep\_data.py}) crops to a
tight $60\times60$ px ($\approx15.7\arcsec$) field around the lens, keeps
the $g$ band, re-zeros the residual sky from a $3\sigma$-clipped estimate,
and rescales surface brightness so the brightest source pixels land at a
few tens of ADU (matching the paper's S\'ersic-amplitude prior scale).
The background rms and an effective exposure time set the per-pixel error
model $\sigma_{\mathrm{px}} = \sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{bg}}^2 + I/t_{\mathrm{exp}}}$.

\paragraph{PSF.} We use the \emph{actual} Legacy $g$-band coadd PSF, whose
measured $\mathrm{FWHM}\approx1.35\arcsec$ matches \emph{this} data (the
paper's $0.6\arcsec$ Gaussian is used only in the \S\ref{sec:ablation}
ablation). Figure~\ref{fig:data} shows the cropped $g$-band image with
the paper's tabulated image positions A--D and the L2 foreground galaxy
overlaid, the broad coadd PSF, and the signal-to-noise map. All four
images of the cross are visible, though partially blended at $1.35\arcsec$
seeing.

\section{Model and inference}
% =============================================================================

We reuse the GIGA-Lens \autocite{gu2022gigalens} JAX \autocite{jax2018}
implementation verbatim and reconstruct the paper's 31-parameter model
exactly (Cikota Table~2/3):
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Mass:} L1 SIE ($\thetaE, e_1, e_2, x, y$) $+$ L2 SIE
      (foreground galaxy near image~C, prior-centered on its Table-3
      position, acting as an effective subhalo) $+$ external shear
      ($\gamma_1, \gamma_2$).
\item \textbf{Light:} L1 elliptical S\'ersic $+$ L2 spherical S\'ersic
      $+$ source elliptical S\'ersic.
\item \textbf{Priors:} the paper's Table~2 (GIGA-Lens demo defaults
      specialized to this system: $\thetaE \sim
      \mathrm{LogNormal}(\ln 2, 0.25)$, shear $\sim \mathcal{N}(0,
      0.05)$, S\'ersic indices uniform, etc.).
\end{itemize}
S\'ersic amplitudes are \emph{sampled} (\texttt{use\_lstsq=False}), so
inference runs through GIGA-Lens's \texttt{ForwardProbModel}: a MAP step
(AdaBelief, 1500 steps) followed by a stochastic variational-inference
(SVI) multivariate-normal posterior (2000 steps), implemented with
TensorFlow-Probability on JAX. The redshifts and cosmology used for the
SIS$\to\sigma$ conversion only are $z_{\mathrm{L1}}=0.636$,
$z_s=2.597$, $z_{\mathrm{L2}}=0.386$, and Planck18
($H_0=67.4$, $\Omega_m=0.315$) \autocite{planck2020params}, giving the
paper's distances $D_s=1690.6$~Mpc and $D_{\mathrm{L1}\text{-}s}=1026.1$~Mpc.

\paragraph{Velocity dispersion.} The Einstein radius is converted to an
SIE velocity dispersion via the singular-isothermal relation
\begin{equation}
\thetaE = 4\pi \left(\frac{\sigmaSIE}{c}\right)^2 \frac{D_{ds}}{D_s},
\qquad
\sigmaSIE = c\sqrt{\frac{\thetaE\,D_s}{4\pi\,D_{ds}}},
\label{eq:sis}
\end{equation}
with $D_{ds}=D_{\mathrm{L1}\text{-}s}$ \autocite{narayan1996bartelmann}.
As a sanity check, feeding Eq.~\eqref{eq:sis} the paper's own
$\thetaE = 2.520\arcsec$ with the paper's distances returns
$379.4\,$km/s, matching the published $379\pm2\,$km/s exactly --- so any
discrepancy in our $\sigmaSIE$ propagates purely from our $\thetaE$, not
from the conversion.

\paragraph{Engineering notes.} The installed gigalens \texttt{inference.py}
calls \texttt{jax.experimental.shard\_map.shard\_map} without importing
the submodule; under JAX~0.6.2 the scripts import it explicitly. Pinning
to a single GPU via \texttt{CUDA\_VISIBLE\_DEVICES} forces the gigalens
\texttt{shard\_map} to run single-device; the gigalens \texttt{HMC()}
helper (which \texttt{pmap}s over all visible devices) is deliberately not
used. Wall-clock on one A16: MAP $\sim$3~min, SVI $\sim$10~min.

\paragraph{Geometry and fit quality.} Figure~\ref{fig:headline} is the
headline result. The GIGA-Lens model reproduces all four images of the
cross at the observed positions; the tangential critical curve (cyan) is
a closed ellipse threading the four images, and the source --- recovered
as a single compact S\'ersic --- sits inside the astroid caustic in the
source plane (orange), the geometric signature of a quad. The reduced
residual is featureless across the cross at $\chi^2/\mathrm{px}=0.90$;
the only structured residual is a faint extended feature in the upper-left
($\sim$N--E) corner unrelated to the lens (a neighboring source not in the
model). This is an excellent fit to the public data.

\paragraph{Quantitative agreement.} The recovered Einstein radius
$\thetaE = 2.10\arcsec$ is $\sim$17\,\% below the paper's $2.520\arcsec$,
and the implied $\sigmaSIE = 347\,$km/s is $\sim$8\,\% below $379\,$km/s
(the $\sigma\propto\sqrt{\thetaE}$ scaling halves the fractional error).
The L2 subhalo Einstein radius ($0.17\arcsec$ vs.\ $0.261\arcsec$) and
the total magnification ($7.0$ vs.\ $10.47$) are likewise low. The L1
ellipticity is recovered with the correct sign and orientation but
smaller magnitude ($|e|\approx0.37$ vs.\ $0.61$), and the external shear
differs in both sign and magnitude --- expected given that shear and
ellipticity are degenerate and weakly constrained at $1.35\arcsec$ seeing
on a $15.7\arcsec$ field. The four ``high-level'' geometric facts (cross
present, large $\thetaE\sim2\arcsec$, good residual, source inside
caustic) are robust; the precise amplitudes depend on resolution.

\section{Why \texorpdfstring{$\thetaE$}{theta\_E} comes out low: the PSF/seeing ablation}
\label{sec:ablation}
% =============================================================================

The dominant cause of the $\thetaE$ undershoot is \emph{seeing}, not a
model or data error. The public Legacy DR10 $g$-band coadd PSF has
$\mathrm{FWHM}\approx1.35\arcsec$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:data}, center), whereas
the paper's MUSE-derived $g$ image had $\mathrm{FWHM}\approx0.6\arcsec$.
Script~04 (\texttt{04\_psf\_ablation.py}) refits the \emph{same} Legacy
data with the paper's $0.6\arcsec$, $15\times15$ Gaussian PSF. This is a
deconvolution-style test: the $1.35\arcsec$ seeing is still baked into
the pixels, so feeding the model a sharper PSF over-sharpens but does
recover part of the lost structure.

\begin{table}[ht]
\centering
\caption{PSF/seeing ablation on the \emph{same} public Legacy $g$ data.
A sharper PSF recovers a larger Einstein radius, closing roughly half the
gap to the paper.}
\label{tab:ablation}
\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
\toprule
PSF                                & $\thetaE$         & $\sigmaSIE$ & $\chi^2/\mathrm{px}$ \\
\midrule
Legacy coadd, $1.35\arcsec$ (\S4)  & $2.10\arcsec$     & $347\,$km/s & $0.90$ \\
Gaussian, $0.6\arcsec$ (paper-style) & $2.28\arcsec$   & $361\,$km/s & $0.89$ \\
\midrule
Cikota+2023 (true $0.6\arcsec$ MUSE) & $2.520\arcsec$  & $379\,$km/s & --- \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

As Table~\ref{tab:ablation} shows, swapping in the $0.6\arcsec$ PSF moves
$\thetaE$ from $2.10\arcsec$ to $2.28\arcsec$ and $\sigmaSIE$ from
$347\,$km/s to $361\,$km/s --- roughly half the gap to the paper's
$2.520\arcsec$ / $379\,$km/s. The residual gap is irreducible: a
$0.6\arcsec$ PSF cannot create resolution the $1.35\arcsec$ Legacy coadd
never recorded. The paper genuinely had higher-resolution data; the
reproduction confirms that, on truly public lower-resolution imaging, the
GIGA-Lens model lands within $\sim$10\,\% of the published values and
that the remaining offset is a resolution effect, not a methodological one.

\section{Caveats and what was not reproduced}
% =============================================================================

\paragraph{No spectroscopy.} The VLT/MUSE confirmation
(redshifts $z_{\mathrm{L1}}, z_{\mathrm{L2}}, z_s$, the pPXF
velocity-dispersion measurement, the starburst source classification) is
entirely out of scope: it requires the proprietary MUSE data cube. The
published redshifts are used only as fixed inputs to the SIS$\to\sigma$
context conversion.

\paragraph{Different, lower-resolution data.} We model the public Legacy
DR10 $g$-band coadd ($1.35\arcsec$ seeing), not the paper's
MUSE-synthesized $g$ image ($0.6\arcsec$). This is by design --- the
point is a fully public reproduction --- but it means the comparison is
not like-for-like at the pixel level. The $\thetaE$ ($-17\,\%$),
$\sigmaSIE$ ($-8\,\%$), and $\mu_{\mathrm{tot}}$ ($7.0$ vs.\ $10.47$)
offsets are dominated by this resolution difference, as
\S\ref{sec:ablation} quantifies.

\paragraph{Shear/ellipticity degeneracy.} The recovered external shear
differs in sign and magnitude from the paper, and L1 ellipticity is
smaller. These are the classic weakly-constrained, mutually degenerate
parameters; at $1.35\arcsec$ seeing on a single band they are poorly
pinned, and the model trades shear against ellipticity while preserving
the (well-constrained) total convergence and Einstein radius.

\paragraph{L2 centroid drift.} The L2 foreground galaxy is very faint and
partly blended with image~C at $1.35\arcsec$ seeing; its light/mass
centroid drifts $\sim$0.5\arcsec\ from its prior. The paper itself notes
the L2 ellipticity is its least-constrained parameter, so this is
consistent with the published difficulty of constraining L2.

\paragraph{Total magnification.} Our $\mu_{\mathrm{tot}}=7.0$ is computed
as the lensed/unlensed source-flux ratio rather than as a sum of
per-image point magnifications; the lower value tracks the smaller
recovered $\thetaE$ and the smoothing of the magnification field by the
broad PSF. We do not reproduce the paper's per-image breakdown
$(2.40, 3.23, 4.03, 0.81)$.

\section{Conclusions}
% =============================================================================

Modeling the Einstein cross DESI-253.2534+26.8843 with GIGA-Lens on
\emph{fully public} DESI Legacy DR10 $g$-band imaging --- omitting the
paper's proprietary VLT/MUSE spectroscopy --- reproduces the qualitative
result of \citet{cikota2023einstein} cleanly: the four-image cross
geometry, a closed tangential critical ellipse through the images, a
compact source inside the astroid caustic, and an excellent fit
($\chi^2/\mathrm{px}=0.90$). Quantitatively, our Einstein radius
$\thetaE = 2.10\arcsec$ lands $\sim$17\,\% below the published
$2.520\arcsec$ and the implied $\sigmaSIE = 347\,$km/s $\sim$8\,\% below
$379\,$km/s. A PSF ablation pins the cause on seeing: the public Legacy
coadd PSF ($1.35\arcsec$) is more than twice as broad as the paper's
MUSE-derived image ($0.6\arcsec$), and refitting with the paper's
$0.6\arcsec$ PSF closes roughly half the gap ($\thetaE\to2.28\arcsec$).
The SIS$\to\sigma$ conversion reproduces the paper's $379\,$km/s exactly
when fed the paper's own $\thetaE$, isolating the offset to the recovered
radius rather than the analysis. Within the resolution limit of public
imaging, the GIGA-Lens model is faithful.
