\subtitle{Final results and lessons; the complete development record is evolution.pdf}

\begin{abstract}
We report the final state of an end-to-end, public-data reproduction of
the \gigalens\ modeling demonstration for the DESI Strong Lens Foundry I
system \foundry\ \citep{huang2025foundry1}. On the corrected data
product --- {\it HST}/WFC3-IR F140W at native scale, with interloper
masks, a model-subtracted noise calibration, and a convention-correct
PSF kernel --- preconditioned HMC at the published compute scale gives
$\gammaEPL = 1.433\,[1.400, 1.468]$, consistent with the published
$1.372\pm0.023$ at $1.5\sigma$ of the combined statistical
uncertainties, with $\thetaE = 2.6551$ within $0.33\%$
of the published value, strict convergence
($\hat R_{\max}=1.077$, $\gammaEPL$ ESS $=5{,}714$), and the published
inner-critical-curve topology recovered. Beyond the statistical error
($\pm0.034$), the slope carries two systematics of comparable size: a
$\sim$$0.1$ model-class term (source/companion flexibility) and a
$\sim$$0.1$ data-scale term (a low/steep-slope bimodality that exists
only on upsampled drizzle products and would require a correlated-noise
likelihood to resolve). Reaching this end state surfaced two findings of
direct relevance to the \gigalens\ stack: simulator PSF kernels must be
sampled at the data pixel scale (a convention violation broadened our
effective PSF $2\times$ and was the entire native-scale misfit floor),
and sky-based noise calibrations are biased by diffuse lens-wing flux
unless calibrated on model-subtracted residuals. We provide the final
parameter comparison, the corrected data/inference recipe, the
uncertainty budget, seven documented upstream issues with fixes, and
the compute cost ($44$ of $200$ budgeted A100-hours).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\citet{huang2025foundry1} demonstrated GPU-accelerated Bayesian lens
modeling with \gigalens\ \citep{gu2022gigalens} on the Foundry I system
\foundry, reporting a full 74-parameter posterior (EPL $+$ external
shear mass model, four-S\'{e}rsic lens light, S\'{e}rsic $+$ shapelet
source) from MAP $\to$ SVI $\to$ preconditioned HMC in $193$ minutes on
one Perlmutter A100 node. This document is the {\bf final report} of an
independent, public-data reproduction of that analysis: it presents the
end state --- the corrected data treatment, the final posterior and its
uncertainty budget, and what the exercise established about the
\gigalens\ code base --- and omits the development history.

The reproduction was developed over thirteen internal versions
(May--June 2026), including an extended investigation of HMC
pathologies, a paper-scale campaign on NERSC Perlmutter, and a
group-review-driven rigor revision that retired several intermediate
claims. That complete record, including every superseded result and the
reasoning that led to each correction, is preserved verbatim in the
companion document \texttt{evolution.pdf}
(\url{https://github.com/usfcs-edu/agentic-lensing/blob/main/reproductions/foundry-i/papers/evolution.pdf});
the campaign execution log is
\texttt{PERLMUTTER\_CAMPAIGN.md} in the repository. Here, superseded
claims appear only as named retractions (\S\ref{sec:defects}).

\subsection{Uncertainty budget for \gammaEPL}
\label{sec:result-budget}

The statistical error on the slope is $\pm0.034$. Two systematics of
comparable or larger size must be quoted with it:

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf Model class ($\sim$$0.1$).} Raising the source shapelet
order to $n_{\max}=8$ and adding a third companion S\'{e}rsic improves
the MAP only marginally ($\chi^{2}_{\nu}=1.234\to1.203$, not plateaued)
but moves the slope from $1.41$ to $1.31$. The published value
($1.372$) lies inside this band.
\item {\bf Data scale ($\sim$$0.1$).} The low-slope solution moves from
$\gammaEPL=1.29$ (binned $0\farcs08$ product) through $1.43$ (native
scale) with the published $1.37$ (at $0\farcs065$) between them. A
sharper version of the same scale dependence appears as an explicit
bimodality on upsampled products (\S\ref{sec:open}).
\end{itemize}

\noindent The defensible summary statement is therefore
$\gammaEPL = 1.43 \pm 0.03\,\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.1\,\mathrm{(syst)}$,
in agreement with the published $1.372\pm0.023$, whose own error bar is
purely statistical.

\subsection{The orientation frame of \etwo\ and \gexttwo}
\label{sec:result-orientation}

The signed \etwo\ and \gexttwo\ disagree with the published catalog
while \eone\ and \gextone\ agree --- the signature of mirrored
position-angle conventions rather than a physics discrepancy. In polar
form our posterior gives $|e| = 0.136$ at mass position angle
$+19\fdg0$ and $|\gext| = 0.081$ at $+14\fdg7$ (in our pixel frame),
versus the published $|e| = 0.171$ at $-25\fdg2$ and $|\gext| = 0.115$
at $-27\fdg5$ (in theirs). Three facts pin this down as a frame
difference. First, the deflector {\it light}, measured directly from
second moments of the image we model, lies at $+25\fdg5$: our mass is
aligned with the light to $6^{\circ}$, as expected physically, whereas
the published angle interpreted in our frame would be $50^{\circ}$ from
the light. Second, under a parity flip (PA $\to-$PA) the published mass
angle maps to $+25\fdg2$ --- within $0\fdg3$ of our measured light
angle. Third, the two reductions model different image products (we
model the MAST HAP mosaic; the paper drizzled its own stack), so a
mirrored axis convention between them is unremarkable. After the
reflection is removed, the genuine residuals are a $6\fdg2$ rotation
of the mass ellipticity, a $12\fdg8$ rotation of the shear, and
$21\%$/$29\%$ smaller $|e|$/$|\gext|$ amplitudes --- a coherent
displacement along the well-known ellipticity--shear coupling, of a
size consistent with the $\pm0.1$-class systematics above.

\section{Data and Corrected Treatment}
\label{sec:data}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\foundry\ was observed with {\it HST}/WFC3-IR in F140W under program
GO--15867 (PI Huang): $1197.7$\,s total in three exposures, public via
MAST DOI 10.17909/hx0v-9260. We model the high-level pipeline-combined
drizzle product
\texttt{hst\_15867\_65\_wfc3\_ir\_f140w\_ie5065\_drz.fits} at its true
scale of $0\farcs12825\,\mathrm{pix}^{-1}$ (the \texttt{D001SCAL}
keyword; the WFC3-IR nominal $0\farcs13$ is a $1.36\%$ relabel), and,
for cross-scale checks, the MAST HAP fine-skycell mosaic of the same
exposures at $0\farcs04\,\mathrm{pix}^{-1}$ and a $2\times2$-binned
$0\farcs08$ rebuild of it.

The final data product (\texttt{cutout\_v2d}, built by
\texttt{40\_make\_cutout\_v2.py} $+$ \texttt{40d\_fix\_v2\_psf.py})
applies three corrections that proved decisive:

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf Crop and masks.} An $80\times80$\,px ($10\farcs4$) crop so
the lens fills the frame; whole-object masks on the three faint field
galaxies; a mask on the compact interloper superposed on arc A
(identified automatically as the most point-like member of the closest
ring-detection pair, leaving all four lensed images unmasked); and
$r\le0\farcs2$ core masks on the lens and companion centers
(Figure~\ref{fig:masks}).
\item {\bf Noise calibrated on model-subtracted sky.} The per-pixel
error map is the drizzle \texttt{WHT}-implied sigma plus a data-based
Poisson term. The sky-term normalization is calibrated so that {\it
model-subtracted} residuals in the source-free annulus have unit
reduced $\chi^{2}$: calibrating on raw image fluctuations --- the
common shortcut --- is biased high by diffuse lens-wing flux that the
model fits ($21\%$ in variance at native scale, $3.3\times$ on the fine
skycell). With the wing bias removed, the \texttt{WHT}-implied noise is
correct at native scale to $1\%$. The Poisson term is verified rather
than assumed: removing it overshoots the arc-region $\chi^{2}$ to
$2.26$, so it is kept.
\item {\bf Convention-correct PSF (\S\ref{sec:psf}).}
\end{itemize}

\noindent Product tags used throughout: {\bf v2c} $=$ native scale with
the PSF fix only (noise as before; isolates the PSF effect); {\bf v2d}
$=$ v2c $+$ the model-subtracted noise calibration (the final product);
{\bf v3} $=$ the $0\farcs04$ fine-skycell cutout; {\bf v3b} $=$ its
$2\times2$-binned $0\farcs08$ rebuild.

\section{The PSF: Construction and the Sampling-Convention Rule}
\label{sec:psf}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Empirical PSF}

The \gigalens-default TinyTim F140W kernel is too compact for this
analysis: it is identically zero beyond $\pm0\farcs42$ where the real
PSF carries measurable power, and replacing it with an empirical PSF
moves the global-best MAP basin into the published parameter quadrant.
Our kernel is built with \texttt{photutils.psf.EPSFBuilder}
\citep{bradley2024photutils} from $30$ isolated in-field stars of the
same drizzle ($2\times$ oversampling, quartic smoothing, eight outer
iterations), cropped to $27\times27$ supersampled pixels
($\pm0\farcs85$) and unit-normalized
(\texttt{17\_build\_empirical\_psf.py}; a native-sampled $51\times51$
variant from $25$ skycell stars serves the fine-scale products). An
in-exposure empirical PSF should be considered a default ingredient for
this kind of modeling, not an enhancement.

\subsection{The sampling-convention rule}
\label{sec:psf-convention}

The single largest defect found in this reproduction was not
astrophysical: \gigalens's simulator passes \texttt{sim\_config.kernel}
through \lenstronomy's \texttt{subgrid\_kernel}, which {\bf assumes the
kernel is sampled at the data pixel scale} (\texttt{delta\_pix}) and
upsamples it internally by the supersampling factor. Supplying our
$0\farcs065$-sampled empirical kernel together with
$\texttt{delta\_pix}=0.13$ and $\texttt{supersample}=2$ therefore
stretched the effective PSF by $2\times$ ($r_{50}$ ratio $1.92$; model
images change by $5.4\sigma$ RMS when corrected). Every native-scale
fit of the development record ran with this broadened kernel.

Refitting the same data with a convention-correct kernel (a
$0\farcs13$-sampled $15\times15$ kernel least-squares-fitted through
\texttt{subgrid\_kernel} itself) and {\it no other change} drops the
native-scale MAP floor from $\chi^{2}_{\nu}=3.42$--$3.55$ to $1.051$
(Figure~\ref{fig:psffix}; Table~\ref{tab:ladder}). It also resolves
what had appeared to be a sampler limitation: the slope's effective
sample size, stuck at $259$ after $13{,}000$ draws per chain under the
broadened kernel, reaches $5{,}714$ from $8{,}000$ draws once the PSF
is corrected --- the broadened kernel, not the sampler, had flattened
the \gammaEPL\ direction.

\section{Lens Model}
\label{sec:model}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We adopt the published parametric model \citep{huang2025foundry1}: a
single elliptical power-law convergence (\texttt{EPL};
\citealt{gu2022gigalens,birrer2018lenstronomy}) with Einstein radius
\thetaE, slope \gammaEPL, ellipticity $(\eone,\etwo)$ and centroid,
plus external shear $(\gextone,\gexttwo)$ --- $8$ mass parameters;
four elliptical S\'{e}rsic profiles for the lens light (two for the
deflector, two for the companion galaxy at
$(-2\farcs34,-2\farcs86)$); and a S\'{e}rsic $+$ Cartesian-shapelet
source ($n_{\max}=6$, $28$ amplitude coefficients with
$\mathcal{N}(0,5/\sqrt{i+1})$ priors). Total: $74$ free parameters;
the full prior table is Appendix~\ref{app:priors}.

Two implementation choices matter. First,
\texttt{use\_lstsq=False} everywhere: \gigalens's unconstrained
least-squares pass for linear amplitudes can drive S\'{e}rsic
intensities negative and absorb $\chi^{2}$ at non-physical
configurations (it degraded Einstein-radius accuracy from $0.5\%$ to
$43\%$ in early ablations), and its \texttt{pinv}-based profiled
posterior is non-smooth (\S\ref{sec:gigalens}). Second, the central
$r\le0\farcs2$ degeneracy mask is implemented by inflating the error
map inside the mask --- the smallest change to \gigalens\ that yields a
masked likelihood, since \texttt{ForwardProbModel} exposes no mask
argument.

\section{Inference: the Recipe That Works at Published Quality}
\label{sec:inference}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The published pipeline recipe --- multi-start MAP, full-covariance
Gaussian SVI, preconditioned HMC with momentum covariance
$\Sigma_{\mathrm{VI}}^{-1}$ --- works as advertised once given adequate
scale and the corrected inputs above. The configuration that reaches
published quality (one Perlmutter node, $4\times$A100, vendored
\texttt{gigalens-sean} \texttt{multinode-2025} library):

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf MAP:} $1{,}000$ particles $\times$ $6{,}000$--$8{,}000$
AdaBelief steps with a polynomial learning-rate decay and a
loss-plateau stop; cold and warm starts agree. Runtime $8$--$26$\,min.
\item {\bf SVI:} $1{,}000$ particles, $15{,}000$ steps,
$\texttt{init\_scales}=3\times10^{-3}$, quadratic learning-rate ramp.
The longer schedule and larger \texttt{init\_scales} are what produce a
{\it full-rank} $74\times74$ variational covariance (a $1{,}500$-step
run yields rank $53/74$, which silently breaks the HMC preconditioner;
\S\ref{sec:gigalens}). ELBO must be smooth and flattened. Runtime
$14$--$55$\,min depending on product size.
\item {\bf HMC:} $48$ chains from SVI draws, ChEES trajectory
adaptation, dual-averaging step size, momentum covariance
$=\Sigma_{\mathrm{VI}}^{-1}$; $750$ burn-in $+$ $8{,}000$ draws in
$10$\,min.
\item {\bf Environment:} single or few devices with
\texttt{XLA\_FLAGS=--xla\_gpu\_autotune\_level=0}; never run this
likelihood on CPU (a grouped-convolution XLA pathology makes it
$216\times$ slower than a single L4).
\end{itemize}

For HMC work outside the \texttt{ModellingSequence} path we also
marginalize the $28$ shapelet amplitudes analytically, using their own
Gaussian priors as a Tikhonov ridge: with $W$ the per-pixel precision,
$X$ the lensed, PSF-convolved unit-amplitude design matrix, and $R$ the
residual after the deterministic components,
\begin{align*}
  A &= X^{\!\top} W X + \Lambda, \qquad
       \Lambda_{ii} = \frac{i+1}{25},\\
  a^{\star} &= \mathrm{cho\_solve}(A,\,X^{\!\top}(W\!\odot R)),\qquad
  \log L = -\tfrac12\!\sum W R^{2}
            + \tfrac12\, b^{\!\top} a^{\star}
            - \tfrac12 \log\det(A),
\end{align*}
where the ridge makes $A$ strictly positive-definite (Cholesky, never
\texttt{pinv}) and $-\tfrac12\log\det A$ is the Gaussian-evidence
(Occam) term that \gigalens's \texttt{lstsq\_simulate} omits. This
single change reduced the gradient floor of the profiled posterior by
$228\times$ and is the fix we recommend upstream
(\S\ref{sec:gigalens}).

the fit quality and sampling diagnostics across data products.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Why Earlier Attempts Disagreed: the Defect Chain}
\label{sec:defects}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The development record's central discrepancy --- a $57\%$-steeper
slope ($\gammaEPL=2.15$) that resisted $119$ GPU-hours of sampling
effort --- dissolved entirely under five corrections, none of them
model physics:

\begin{enumerate}
\item {\bf Uncropped field}: modeling a $128^{2}$ cutout left the fit
free to spend flexibility on field structure; the corrected product
crops to $80^{2}$ so the lens fills the frame.
\item {\bf Unmasked interlopers}: three faint galaxies and a compact
object superposed on arc A were previously fit rather than masked.
\item {\bf Under-invested optimization}: early MAP/SVI used
$\sim$$25\times$ fewer particle-steps than the published runs;
at paper scale the same code converges cleanly.
\item {\bf Wing-contaminated noise calibration}: sky-based $\sigma$
calibrations on raw image fluctuations were inflated by diffuse
lens-wing flux. Two intermediate claims are retracted accordingly: an
apparent ``\texttt{WHT} underestimates sky noise by $25\%$'' finding
(rescale $1.117$) was an artifact of calibrating on wing-contaminated
raw sky, not a \texttt{WHT} defect --- recalibrated on model-subtracted
residuals (variance factor $0.787$), the drizzle-weight noise is correct
to $1\%$ ($1.117\times\sqrt{0.787}=0.99$); and a fine-scale
``$\chi^{2}_{\nu}=0.451$''
quoted as meeting the fit-quality bar (recalibrated: $0.915$ --- which
{\it does} meet it, with a calibrated statistic;
Figure~\ref{fig:audit}).
\item {\bf The PSF sampling-convention defect}
(\S\ref{sec:psf-convention}): the entire remaining native-scale misfit
floor, and the apparent \gammaEPL-mixing pathology, were the
$2\times$-broadened kernel.
\end{enumerate}

An earlier hypothesis that the residual parameter gaps indicated a
missing physical constraint (a mass--light position-angle tie or a
higher-order multipole) is retracted: the published model contains
neither --- its Table~2 priors are fully independent --- and the gap
closed through items 1--5 alone. Likewise, soft posterior directions
once labeled ``mass-sheet / slope--ellipticity degeneracies'' are more
honestly described as a broad valley coupling \gammaEPL, $\eone/\etwo$,
and \gext\ under the pre-correction likelihood; the valley largely
closed once the likelihood was fixed.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Open Findings}
\label{sec:open}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\paragraph{Scale-dependent slope bimodality.} On drizzle products
upsampled beyond the native scale, a steep-slope solution
($\gammaEPL\approx2.4$--$2.7$) coexists with the low-slope one. On the
$2\times2$-binned $0\farcs08$ product the steep basin retains a
$\Delta\chi^{2}_{\nu}=0.078$ likelihood preference even after the
corrected calibration, and HMC demonstrates the disconnection
directly: $45$ of $48$ chains sample the low mode, $3$ the steep mode,
with zero inter-basin migrations in $3{,}500$ draws. At native scale
the steep mode is not even metastable --- a MAP started from the
fine-scale steep solution drains monotonically to
$\gammaEPL\approx1.17$. The bimodality is therefore a property of
upsampled products (Figure~\ref{fig:fig8v3b} shows the binned-product
low-basin fit), most plausibly through the interaction of the
mis-weighted independent-pixel likelihood with sub-native-pixel
structure.

\paragraph{Pixel correlation, quantified.} Background-detrended sky
autocorrelation of the $0\farcs04$ skycell gives a correlation length
of $2.5$ fine pixels --- the $3.2\times$ upsampling footprint --- and
direct binning shows $2\times2$ fine-pixel blocks are $\sim$$78\%$
internally correlated. The fine grid carries between $\sim$$1/10$ and
$\sim$$1/60$ as many independent elements as pixels
(convention-dependent), so per-pixel statistics on upsampled products
overstate the information content even when the marginal variance is
calibrated.

\paragraph{The methodological need.} Both findings point at the same
missing ingredient: a correlated-noise (pixel-covariance) likelihood.
With it, the fine- and native-scale posteriors should unify and the
bimodality should resolve; without it, native-scale modeling is the
defensible choice, which is why the headline posterior comes from
\texttt{cutout\_v2d}.

\paragraph{Companion structure.} The residual native-scale floor
($\chi^{2}_{\nu}=1.23$ versus the sky-calibrated $1.0$) localizes at
the companion galaxy and is only marginally improved by added
S\'{e}rsic flexibility; a structurally richer companion model is the
natural next increment.

\section{Guidance for the \gigalens\ Code Base}
\label{sec:gigalens}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Seven issues in the \gigalens\,2.0 / JAX\,0.6.2 / TFP\,0.25.0 stack
were documented at a reproducible level during this work (full detail
in \texttt{evolution.pdf}, \S{\it Upstream issues}):

\begin{enumerate}
\item {\bf Implicit \texttt{shard\_map} import} --- attribute access
without importing \texttt{jax.experimental.shard\_map} fails under
JAX 0.6.2; callers must pre-import it.
\item {\bf Negative lens-light amplitudes via unconstrained
\texttt{lstsq}} --- the least-squares amplitude path can return
unphysical negative intensities that absorb $\chi^{2}$;
\texttt{use\_lstsq=False} (or an NNLS variant upstream) avoids it.
\item {\bf The multi-hour HMC ``compile''} is a
\texttt{pmap}$\times$grouped-convolution$\times$cuDNN-autotuner
pathology, not compilation: single/few devices with
\texttt{--xla\_gpu\_autotune\_level=0} compile the same kernel in
$31$--$38$\,s.
\item {\bf \texttt{lstsq\_simulate} omits the Gaussian-evidence term
and is unregularized}, making the profiled posterior non-smooth on
near-rank-deficient shapelet design matrices. The fix
(\S\ref{sec:inference}) is a prior-derived Tikhonov ridge plus the
$-\tfrac12\log\det$ term.
\item {\bf Mixed-GPU-type \texttt{shard\_map} crash} on heterogeneous
hosts; UUID-pinning is the workaround.
\item {\bf \texttt{jnp.linalg.cholesky} silently returns NaN} on a
rank-deficient SVI covariance, producing stuck-looking chains;
defensive jitter (or a NaN-trace warning) is needed wherever an SVI
covariance feeds a TFP preconditioner.
\item {\bf \texttt{DualAveragingStepSizeAdaptation} collapses} on
multimodal-Hessian targets; windowed mass-matrix adaptation would
require a \texttt{JointDistribution} refactor of the prob model.
\end{enumerate}

Beyond bug-level issues, three integration rules emerged that belong
in \gigalens\ documentation (or asserts): {\bf (a)} the PSF kernel
passed to the simulator must be sampled at \texttt{delta\_pix} ---
\texttt{subgrid\_kernel} upsamples it internally, and a
supersampled-scale kernel silently broadens the model PSF
(\S\ref{sec:psf-convention}); {\bf (b)} SVI must be run long enough,
with large enough \texttt{init\_scales}, to produce a full-rank
covariance before HMC preconditioning --- rank deficiency fails
silently through issue 6; {\bf (c)} noise maps calibrated from sky
fluctuations should use model-subtracted residuals, or diffuse lens
light biases the likelihood weight (\S\ref{sec:defects}, item 4). An
in-exposure empirical PSF and a multi-start MAP with basin reporting
are reasonable default additions to the published recipe.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{enumerate}
\item The published Foundry-I analysis reproduces from public data at
published sampling quality: $\thetaE$ to $0.33\%$, $\gammaEPL$
consistent at $1.5\sigma$ of the combined statistical uncertainties
($1.433\,[1.400,1.468]$ vs.\
$1.372\pm0.023$), mass orientation aligned with the measured deflector
light to $6^{\circ}$ (modulo a mirrored PA frame between the two
reductions), and the inner-critical-curve topology recovered.
\item The honest error on the slope is systematics-dominated:
$\pm0.03$ statistical, $\sim$$\pm0.1$ model-class, $\sim$$\pm0.1$
data-scale. Statistical-only error bars understate the uncertainty of
this kind of single-band analysis by a factor of a few.
\item Every discrepancy encountered along the way --- including a
$57\%$-steeper slope that once looked like missing model physics ---
traced to data treatment or software convention: crop, masks,
optimization scale, wing-contaminated noise calibration, and a PSF
sampling-convention defect. The corrections are all recipe-level and
are documented here and in the scripts.
\item Two contributions are directly actionable upstream: the PSF
kernel-sampling rule (with the $2\times$-broadening failure mode as
motivation) and the regularized exact marginalization for linear
amplitudes (ridge $+$ evidence term), alongside seven documented
stack issues.
\item One methodological item remains open: a correlated-noise
likelihood for drizzled products, which would unify the scale-dependent
posteriors and resolve the upsampled-product slope bimodality.
\end{enumerate}
